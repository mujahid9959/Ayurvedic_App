Overview

Neem (Azadirachta indica) is a medicinal tree native to India, often referred to as the "Village Pharmacy" for its wide range of therapeutic applications in Ayurveda.

Benefits

  • Skin Health: Treats acne, eczema, and infections with its antibacterial and antifungal properties.
  • Boosts Immunity: Strengthens the immune system and detoxifies the body.
  • Oral Health: Promotes healthy teeth and gums; neem twigs are used as natural toothbrushes.
  • Blood Purification: Helps cleanse the blood and maintain healthy skin.
  • Pest Control: Acts as a natural insecticide in agriculture.
  • Low maintenance after establishment.
  • High demand for neem-based products in agriculture, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics.
  • Neem trees are drought-tolerant and improve soil fertility.

Uses

  • Neem oil for skin and hair care.
  • Neem leaves in teas or powders for internal detoxification.
  • Neem extracts in Ayurvedic medicines for managing diabetes and inflammation.

Number of Trees

    Plant Spacing:

  • Recommended spacing: 5m × 5m (16.4 ft × 16.4 ft).
  • Allows adequate growth and canopy spread.
  • Trees per Acre: ~170–200 neem trees.
  • Trees per Acre=Area in Square Feet/Spacing
  • For 16.4 ft × 16.4 ft spacing:
  • 43,560/16.4x16.4=170

Cost Breakdown

Saplings for Planting:

  • Cost per Neem Sapling: ₹10–₹20 (varies by nursery and location).
  • Total Cost of Saplings: ₹10 × 170 = ₹1,700 (minimum) ₹20 × 200 = ₹4,000 (maximum).

    • Land Preparation:

  • Land Clearing and Leveling: ₹5,000–₹10,000.
  • Pitting and Planting Holes: 200 pits (2x2x2 ft) at ₹10–₹20 per pit = ₹2,000–₹4,000.

    • Manure and Fertilizers:

  • Organic Manure: 1–2 kg per pit = 200–400 kg.
  • Cost: ₹1,000–₹1,500.
  • Fertilizers (optional): ₹1,000–₹2,000.

    • Irrigation Setup:

  • Neem trees require minimal irrigation but benefit from drip irrigation during the initial growth period.
  • Drip Irrigation Setup (optional): ₹20,000–₹30,000.

    • Labor Costs:

  • Planting, Weeding, and Maintenance: ₹10,000–₹15,000.

Maintenance Costs (Yearly)

  • Weeding and Fertilizer Application: ₹5,000–₹8,000 per year.
  • Irrigation (if needed): ₹2,000–₹4,000 annually (optional).

Yield and Revenue

    Neem trees are a long-term investment, typically starting to yield products after 5–7 years.

    Products and Potential Revenue:

  • Neem Seeds: Yield starts after ~5 years, with each tree producing 20–50 kg per year.
  • 170 trees × 30 kg (average) = 5,100 kg of neem seeds.
  • Market Price for Seeds: ₹15–₹30 per kg.
  • Revenue from Seeds: ₹76,500–₹1,53,000 per year.
  • Neem Oil (if processed): Neem seeds contain ~20–30% oil.
  • Processing oil increases revenue significantly.
  • Timber and Leaves: Used for herbal products, biopesticides, and firewood.

    • Long-term Revenue Potential:

  • From the 5th year onwards, neem trees can generate ₹1–₹2 lakh per acre annually, depending on market demand and processing capabilities.

Profit Estimation

  • Initial Investment: ₹40,700–₹66,500.
  • Revenue after 5 years: ₹76,500–₹1,53,000 annually.
  • Net Profit (after Year 5): Subtracting maintenance, profit could range from ₹60,000–₹1,40,000 per year.
