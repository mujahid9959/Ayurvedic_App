Overview

Tulsi (Holy Basil) is an aromatic herb highly revered in Hinduism. Known for its medicinal properties, it is widely used in Ayurvedic medicine.

Benefits

  • Boosts Immunity: Tulsi strengthens the immune system, helping fight infections.
  • Reduces Stress: Known as an adaptogen, it helps the body cope with stress.
  • Improves Respiratory Health: Tulsi has been used to treat cough, cold, and asthma.
  • Antioxidant Properties: Protects the body from free radical damage and supports overall health.

Uses

  • Herbal teas and infusions for its calming effects.
  • Ayurvedic treatments for improving immunity and balancing the body.
  • Skincare products due to its anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Relieving stress and improving mental clarity.

Number of Plants

Tulsi is generally grown from seeds or cuttings, and the planting density varies based on the desired spacing. Here are some common planting densities:

    Spacing:

  • For direct sowing (seeds): 6–8 inches between plants, 18–24 inches between rows.
  • For transplanting seedlings: 6–8 inches between plants, 18–24 inches between rows.
  • Plants per Acre: You can plant approximately 40,000 to 50,000 plants per acre.
  • Area in Square Feet/Spacing=43,560/Spacing in ft
  • With 6–8 inches spacing between plants, you'll get around 40,000 to 50,000 plants per acre.

Yield and Revenue

  • Time to Harvest: 2–3 months (Tulsi can be harvested multiple times in a year, typically 2–3 harvests).
  • Yield per Acre: 2–3 tons of fresh Tulsi leaves (depending on care).
  • Market Price: ₹50–₹100 per kg (fresh leaves).

    • Revenue Potential:

  • 2 tons of leaves: 2,000 kg × ₹50 = ₹1,00,000 (minimum)
  • 3 tons of leaves: 3,000 kg × ₹100 = ₹3,00,000 (maximum)

    • Revenue from Essential Oils (if distillation is considered):

  • Essential Oil Yield: ~0.5–1 kg of essential oil per acre.
  • Market Price for Tulsi Essential Oil: ₹5,000–₹10,000 per kg.

    • Revenue from Essential Oils:

  • 0.5 kg: ₹5,000 (minimum)
