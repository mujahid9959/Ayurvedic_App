Overview

Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera), also known as Indian Ginseng or Winter Cherry, is a key herb in Ayurveda, valued for its adaptogenic properties that help the body manage stress.

Benefits

  • Reduces Stress and Anxiety: Known for its calming effects by lowering cortisol levels.
  • Enhances Energy and Stamina: Improves physical performance and reduces fatigue.
  • Boosts Immunity: Strengthens the immune system and supports overall health.
  • Improves Brain Function: Enhances memory, focus, and cognitive abilities.
  • Supports Hormonal Balance: Beneficial for reproductive health in both men and women.

Uses

  • Commonly consumed as a powder or capsule to reduce stress and improve vitality.
  • Used in Ayurvedic tonics to treat weakness, insomnia, and chronic fatigue.

Number of Plants

    Plant Spacing:

  • Rows: 1.5–2 feet apart
  • Plants within rows: 1–1.5 feet apart
  • Plants per Acre: ~12,000–15,000 plants
  • Plants per Acre=Area in Square Feet/Spacing
  • For 1.5 ft x 1 ft spacing:

    • 43,560/1.5x1=29,040

    After accounting for pathways, approximately 12,000–15,000 plants can be accommodated.

Cost Breakdown

Seeds for Planting:

  • Seed Requirement: ~5–6 kg per acre.
  • Cost per kg: ₹500–₹800.
  • Total Seed Cost: ₹2,500–₹4,800.

    • Land Preparation:

  • Land Clearing and Tilling: ₹8,000–₹12,000.
  • Bed Preparation: ₹2,000–₹3,000.

    • Manure and Fertilizers:

  • Organic Manure/Compost: 5–8 tons per acre (₹1,000–₹1,500 per ton).
  • Fertilizers: ₹5,000–₹8,000.
  • Total Manure and Fertilizer Cost: ₹10,000–₹15,000.

    • Irrigation Setup:

  • Ashwagandha is drought-tolerant but benefits from minimal irrigation.
  • Drip Irrigation Setup (optional): ₹20,000–₹30,000.

    • Labor Costs:

  • Planting, Weeding, and Maintenance: ₹8,000–₹12,000.

Maintenance Costs (Yearly)

  • Weeding and Pest Control: ₹3,000–₹5,000.
  • Irrigation (if needed): ₹2,000–₹4,000.

Yield and Revenue

  • Time to Harvest: 5–6 months after planting.
  • Yield per Acre: ~6–7 quintals of dried roots (600–700 kg).
  • Market Price: ₹100–₹150 per kg (varies by quality and region).

    • Revenue Potential:

  • Minimum: 600 kg × ₹100 = ₹60,000.
  • Maximum: 700 kg × ₹150 = ₹1,05,000.

Profit Estimation

    Net Profit (after 1 year): Revenue - Total Costs

  • Minimum: ₹60,000 – ₹50,500 = ₹9,500.
  • Maximum: ₹1,05,000 – ₹76,800 = ₹28,200.

Additional Considerations

  • Government Subsidies: Some agricultural departments or AYUSH programs may offer financial assistance for cultivating medicinal plants like Ashwagandha.
  • Processing Opportunities: Adding value through drying and powdering Ashwagandha roots can increase profits.
