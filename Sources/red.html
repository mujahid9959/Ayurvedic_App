Overview

Red Sandalwood, scientifically known as Pterocarpus santalinus, is a highly valuable and slow-growing tree native to India. It is renowned for its rich red heartwood and various applications in medicine, cosmetics, and furniture-making. Here's a detailed overview

Benefits

  • Improves skin health and complexion.
  • Has anti-inflammatory properties for soothing irritation.
  • Supports wound healing and reduces scars.
  • Acts as a natural coolant for the body.

Uses

Red Sandalwood is used in various ways, including:

  • Face masks and scrubs for glowing skin.
  • Herbal teas to cool the body.
  • Powder form for treating acne and rashes.

Properties

Red Sandalwood is known for its:

  • Cooling and soothing properties.
  • Antioxidant content that promotes skin repair.
  • Anti-inflammatory and antibacterial effects.

Precautions

Although Red Sandalwood is generally safe, consider the following precautions:

  • Avoid using on open wounds without proper guidance.
  • Patch-test before applying to skin to avoid allergies.
  • Consult a healthcare professional if using medicinally.

Number of Plants

The standard planting density for Red Sandalwood trees is 400–500 plants per acre, depending on the spacing:

  • Spacing: 10 ft x 10 ft (recommended for optimal growth)
  • Plants per Acre: 435 plants
  • 10x10 feet spacing allows 435 plants per acre.
  • 12x12 feet spacing allows 302 plants per acre.
