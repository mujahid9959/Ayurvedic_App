Overview

Sandalwood (Santalum album) is a highly revered tree in Ayurveda, known for its aromatic and therapeutic properties. It has been used for centuries in skincare, rituals, and traditional medicine.

Benefits

  • Promotes glowing skin and reduces blemishes.
  • Calms and soothes the mind, reducing anxiety.
  • Acts as an anti-inflammatory agent for skin conditions.
  • Boosts immune health when used in tonics.

Uses

  • Used in skincare products like masks and creams.
  • Essential oil for aromatherapy and relaxation.
  • Incorporated into incense and rituals for purification.
  • Used as a natural remedy for minor wounds and burns.

Properties

Sandalwood is known for its:

  • Aromatic and calming scent.
  • Anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
  • Cooling properties that soothe the skin.

Precautions

Although Sandalwood is safe for most people, consider the following:

  • Perform a patch test before applying directly to the skin.
  • Avoid overusing essential oil to prevent irritation.
  • Consult a healthcare provider before ingesting sandalwood.

Number of Plants

The planting density for sandalwood depends on spacing and intercropping. Common spacing is 10 ft x 10 ft, allowing around 435 trees per acre.

  • Spacing: 10 ft x 10 ft (for optimal growth and canopy spread)
  • Plants per Acre: 435

Cost Breakdown

Sapling Cost:

  • Cost per Sapling: ₹50–₹150 (varies by region and nursery quality)

    • Total Sapling Cost:

  • ₹50 × 435 = ₹21,750 (minimum)
  • ₹150 × 435 = ₹65,250 (maximum)

    • Land Preparation:

  • Land clearing, plowing, and leveling: ₹10,000–₹20,000
  • Pit digging (size: 2x2x2 feet): ₹5–₹10 per pit Total: ₹2,175–₹4,350

    • Manure and Fertilizers:

  • Organic manure (e.g., vermicompost) and fertilizers for 1 acre: ₹5,000–₹10,000

    • Irrigation System:

  • Sandalwood requires consistent irrigation, especially during early growth.
  • Drip irrigation setup: ₹30,000–₹50,000 (one-time investment)

    • Labor Costs:

  • Planting, staking, and initial maintenance: ₹10,000–₹15,000

    • Host Plants:

    Sandalwood is a partial root parasite that requires host plants like Sesbania, Castor, or Red Gram for nutrient uptake.

  • Host plant cost: ₹5,000–₹8,000

Maintenance Costs (Yearly)

  • Weeding, irrigation, and fertilization: ₹15,000–₹20,000 per year.
  • Pruning and pest management: ₹5,000–₹10,000 per year.

Time to Harvest and Revenue

  • Yield per Tree: 15–20 kg of heartwood.
