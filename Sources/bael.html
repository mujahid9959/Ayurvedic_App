Overview

The Bael tree (Aegle marmelos), also known as wood apple, stone apple, or Bilva, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rutaceae family. Native to India and Southeast Asia, it is deeply rooted in culture, religion, and traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions and is known for its resilience and versatile uses.

Benefits

  • Drought-Tolerant: Suitable for dry regions with minimal irrigation.
  • Medicinal and Market Value: High demand for fruits in the herbal, food, and Ayurvedic industries.
  • Bael fruit is a good source of vitamins (A, C, and B complex), minerals, and dietary fiber.
  • Bael pulp is known to treat diarrhea, constipation, and other digestive disorders due to its high tannin content and mild laxative properties.
  • The fruit and leaves have antioxidants that help strengthen the immune system and protect against infections.
  • Bael leaves and fruit extracts are used to manage diabetes by regulating blood sugar levels.
  • Bael has hepatoprotective properties, aiding in detoxification and improving liver function.
  • The leaves, fruit, and bark have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, helping combat infections and inflammation.

Uses

  • Consumed as a powder or capsule for reproductive health and overall wellness.
  • Used in Ayurvedic formulations for postpartum recovery and lactation support.
  • Contains saponins and phytoestrogens, which have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and rejuvenating effects.

Number of Plants

    Plant Spacing:

  • Ideal spacing is 5m × 5m (16.4 ft × 16.4 ft).
  • This spacing ensures adequate room for canopy development and root growth.
  • Trees per Acre: ~160–170 trees.
  • Trees per Acre=Area in Square Feet/Spacing
  • For 16.4 ft × 16.4 ft spacing:
  • 43,560/16.4x16.4=160

Cost Breakdown

Saplings for Planting:

  • Cost per Bael Sapling: ₹20–₹50 (depending on quality and location).
  • Total Cost of Saplings: ₹20 × 160 = ₹3,200 (minimum) ₹50 × 170 = ₹8,500 (maximum).

    • Land Preparation:

  • Land Clearing and Plowing: ₹5,000–₹8,000.
  • Pit Digging (2x2x2 ft): ~170 pits at ₹10–₹20 per pit = ₹1,700–₹3,400.

    • Manure and Fertilizers:

  • Organic Manure (5–10 kg per pit): ~1–2 tons of manure.
  • Cost: ₹1,000–₹1,500 per ton.
  • Fertilizers (optional): ₹2,000–₹3,000.
  • Total Manure and Fertilizer Cost: ₹3,000–₹5,000.

    • Irrigation Setup:

  • Drip Irrigation Setup: ₹20,000–₹30,000 (one-time investment).

    • Labor Costs:

  • Planting, Weeding, and Maintenance: ₹8,000–₹12,000.

Maintenance Costs (Yearly)

  • Weeding and Pest Control: ₹5,000–₹8,000.
  • Irrigation: ₹2,000–₹5,000 annually.

Yield and Revenue

  • Time to Yield: Bael trees start fruiting after 4–5 years.
  • Full yield potential is reached by 8–10 years.
  • Yield per Tree: 100–150 fruits per tree annually at maturity.
  • Average fruit weight: 1–2 kg.
  • Yield per tree: ~100–300 kg.
  • Yield per Acre: ~16,000–48,000 kg (160 trees).
  • Assumes 100–300 kg per tree.
  • Market Price: ₹10–₹30 per kg (depends on quality and region).

    • Revenue Potential:

  • Minimum: 16,000 kg × ₹10 = ₹1,60,000 per year.
  • Maximum: 48,000 kg × ₹30 = ₹14,40,000 per year.

Profit Estimation

    • Net Profit (Year 5 onward):
