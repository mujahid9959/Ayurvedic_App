Overview

Shatavari (Asparagus racemosus) is an important herb in Ayurveda, known as the "Queen of Herbs." It is primarily used to support female reproductive health and promote overall vitality.

Benefits

  • Supports Female Health: Helps regulate menstrual cycles, manage PMS symptoms, and support fertility.
  • Hormonal Balance: Acts as a natural adaptogen, aiding in hormonal regulation.
  • Boosts Immunity: Enhances the body's defense system and fights infections.
  • Improves Digestion: Helps in treating acid reflux, ulcers, and general digestive issues.
  • Anti-Aging Properties: Promotes healthy skin and combats oxidative stress.

Uses

  • Consumed as a powder or capsule for reproductive health and overall wellness.
  • Used in Ayurvedic formulations for postpartum recovery and lactation support.
  • Contains saponins and phytoestrogens, which have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and rejuvenating effects.

Cost Breakdown

Seedlings or Rhizomes:

  • Seedlings Required: ~8,000–10,000 per acre.
  • Cost per Seedling/Rhizome: ₹5–₹10.
  • Total Seedling Cost: ₹40,000–₹1,00,000.

    • Land Preparation:

  • Land Clearing and Plowing: ₹8,000–₹12,000.
  • Bed Preparation: ₹2,000–₹3,000.

    • Manure and Fertilizers:

  • Organic Manure/Compost: 8–10 tons per acre (₹1,000–₹1,500 per ton).
  • Fertilizers: ₹5,000–₹10,000.
  • Total Manure and Fertilizer Cost: ₹13,000–₹25,000.

    • Irrigation Setup:

  • Shatavari thrives in well-drained soil and requires adequate moisture.
  • Drip Irrigation Setup: ₹20,000–₹30,000 (one-time cost).

    • Labor Costs:

  • Planting, Weeding, and Maintenance: ₹15,000–₹20,000.

Maintenance Costs (Yearly)

  • Weeding and Pest Control: ₹5,000–₹8,000.
  • Irrigation and Fertilizer Application: ₹5,000–₹10,000.

Yield and Revenue

  • Time to Harvest: ~1.5–2 years for roots to mature.
  • Yield per Acre: 3–5 tons of dried roots (3,000–5,000 kg).
  • Market Price: ₹150–₹300 per kg (varies by quality and region).

    • Revenue Potential:

  • Minimum: 3,000 kg × ₹150 = ₹4,50,000.
  • Maximum: 5,000 kg × ₹300 = ₹15,00,000.

Profit Estimation

    Net Profit (after 1.5–2 years): Revenue - Total Costs

  • Minimum: ₹4,50,000 – ₹98,000 = ₹3,52,000.
  • Maximum: ₹15,00,000 – ₹1,90,000 = ₹13,10,000.

Additional Information

  • Climatic Requirements: Shatavari thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, requiring well-drained sandy loam soil.
  • Government Subsidies: Check local agricultural or AYUSH departments for financial support under medicinal plant cultivation schemes.
  • Market Demand: Shatavari roots are in high demand in Ayurvedic and herbal industries, ensuring good marketability.
